First-time Setup

When you open Scheduling Assistant for the first time, you will be asked to set up a database. This is the file where all your data — courses, instructors, sections — is stored. It lives on your computer as a single file you can back up like any other document.

Creating a new database

  1. After the splash screen, a Database Location dialog appears. Click Create new database….
  2. Type a name for your database in the Database name field (for example, MyUniversity).
  3. Click Browse… next to Save in folder and choose where the file should live. A Documents or shared drive folder works well.
  4. The full file path is shown as a preview. When it looks right, click OK.
Tip: Choose a folder you back up regularly. The entire database is a single .db file — copying it is all you need to back up your work.

Opening an existing database

If your administrator has set up a database file already, click Open existing database… instead, click Browse…, and locate the .db file.

Switching databases later

Use the Files menu at the top left of the main window. You can open a different database, create a new one, or pick from the list of recently opened databases.

Academic Years & Semesters

Scheduling Assistant organises your data by academic year and semester. Create these first, before adding sections.

Adding an academic year

  1. Click Academic Years in the menu bar. A panel drops down from the top.
  2. Click Add.
  3. Type the name of the year — for example 2025-2026 — and click Save.

Adding a semester

  1. Click Academic Years, then click Semesters in the sidebar (or navigate to the Semesters management screen).
  2. Click Add.
  3. Type a name such as Fall 2025 or Spring 2026, and choose which academic year it belongs to.
  4. Click Save.

Selecting the active semester

Use the two dropdowns in the top-right corner of the window. First choose an Academic Year; the Semester dropdown will update to show only that year's semesters. All three panels immediately reflect your selection.

Tip: You can have many semesters defined at once and switch between them freely — each is a completely separate schedule.

Subjects & Courses

A subject is the broad area of study (e.g. Mathematics, History). A course belongs to a subject and carries a number (e.g. HIST 101 — Introduction to History). Sections are always attached to a course.

Adding a subject

  1. Click Courses in the menu bar.
  2. In the Subjects section, click Add Subject.
  3. Enter the full name (e.g. History) and the calendar abbreviation used on timetables (e.g. HIST).
  4. Click Save.

Adding a course

  1. Click Courses in the menu bar.
  2. Select the subject this course belongs to in the Subjects list above.
  3. In the Courses section, click Add Course.
  4. Choose the Subject from the dropdown, enter a Course Number (three digits, e.g. 101), and a Title.
  5. The Calendar Code is computed automatically (e.g. HIST 101).
  6. Check Active if this course is currently offered, then click Save.
Tip: Inactive courses still appear in dropdowns so that historical sections remain complete, but you can hide them from new sections by marking them inactive.

Instructors

Instructors are the faculty members who teach sections. Adding them here makes them available for assignment when you build sections.

Adding an instructor

  1. Click Instructors in the menu bar.
  2. Click Add.
  3. Fill in First Name, Last Name, and Initials (the initials appear on tiles in the Schedule Grid).
  4. Optionally choose a Staff Type (e.g. Full-time, Adjunct) if you have those configured.
  5. Optionally add Email and Notes.
  6. Leave Active checked for current instructors. Click Save.

Instructor workload

The middle column of the Instructor panel shows the selected instructor's current workload for the active semester: which sections they are assigned to, any non-instructional releases (committee work, research release, etc.), and a total.

Adding a non-instructional release

  1. Select the instructor in the list.
  2. In the Non-instructional Workload column on the right, click Add.
  3. Enter a Title (e.g. Department Chair Release) and a Workload value.
  4. Click Save.
Tip: Workload values throughout the app use a decimal format (e.g. 1.0 for a normal section, 0.5 for a half-credit release). What these numbers mean is up to your institution.

Rooms

Rooms are the physical spaces where sections meet. Adding them here makes them available when you schedule meeting times for a section.

Adding a room

  1. Click Rooms in the menu bar (you will find it under the Courses flyout).
  2. Click Add.
  3. Enter Building, Room Number, and optionally Capacity and Features (e.g. Lab, Projector).
  4. Click Save.

Scheduling Settings

Before you begin adding sections, it's worth setting up the legal meeting times your institution uses. This controls what start times appear in the section editor's dropdowns.

Setting up legal start times

  1. Click Settings → Scheduling in the menu bar.
  2. The list shows block lengths (e.g. 1.0 hour, 1.5 hours) and the valid start times for each.
  3. To add a new block length, click Add. Enter the duration in hours and then add each valid start time (type a time like 8:30 or 1430 and click Add for each).
  4. Click Save when done.

Block patterns

Block patterns are shortcut presets for common day combinations (e.g. "Mon/Wed/Fri" or "Tue/Thu"). You can define up to five. When editing a section's schedule, clicking a pattern button automatically sets the days for each meeting in one click.

  1. Click Settings → Block Patterns.
  2. Click Edit on one of the five slots.
  3. Give it a Name (e.g. MWF) and check the relevant Days.
  4. Click Save.

Other preferences

  • Preferred block length — sets the default block length that appears when you add a new meeting time in the section editor.
  • Include Saturday — check this if your institution schedules Saturday meetings. Saturday will appear in day dropdowns. Changes take effect when you next open the section editor.

Adding a Section

A section is a specific offering of a course in a given semester — for example, HIST 101, Section A, taught by Prof. Smith on Monday and Wednesday mornings.

Before you begin: Make sure you have at least one subject, one course, and an active semester selected in the top-right dropdowns. The section editor will not function without these.
  1. In the Section View panel (left), click the Add button in the header.
  2. A blank edit form appears at the top of the section list with a blue left border.
  3. Select a Subject from the first dropdown. The Course dropdown activates.
  4. Select a Course from the second dropdown. The Section Code box activates.
  5. Type a Section Code (e.g. A or 01). This must be unique for this course in this semester.
  6. Click somewhere else (or press Tab) to validate the code. If another section already uses that code, an error appears — change the code and try again.
  7. Once validated, all remaining fields unlock. Continue filling in the section (see Editing a Section below for field details).
  8. Click Save when finished.
Why the two-step unlock? The course and section code together uniquely identify the section. The app enforces that this combination is set and valid before letting you fill in any other details, so you never accidentally save a section without an identity.

To edit an existing section, click the expand arrow (▶) next to it in the Section View list, or click the Edit button in the panel header while the section is selected. The inline form expands below the section's summary line.

Assigning instructors

  1. Click the Instructor(s) toggle button. A popup opens showing all instructors.
  2. Check the box beside each instructor you want to assign.
  3. A workload field appears next to each selected instructor. Enter a value (default is 1). Leave it blank to accept the default.
  4. Click elsewhere to close the popup. The toggle button updates to show the assigned initials.

Assigning tags, reserves, and resources

These all work the same way as instructor assignment: click the toggle button to open a popup with checkboxes, make your selections, and click elsewhere to close.

Scheduling Meeting Times

A section can have one or more meeting times — for example, a lecture on Monday and Wednesday plus a lab on Friday.

Adding a meeting time

  1. In the section editor, find the Schedule area. Click + meeting to add a row.
  2. Choose the Day, the Block Length (duration in hours), and the Start time from the dropdowns. Only legal start times for the chosen block length are shown.
  3. Optionally choose a Meeting Type and a Room.
  4. To add another meeting, click + meeting again and fill in the new row.
  5. To remove a meeting, click the ✕ button on that row.

Using block patterns

If you defined block patterns in Settings, buttons for each pattern appear near the + meeting button. Clicking a pattern (e.g. MWF) adds a meeting row for each day in that pattern in one click, saving you from adding them one by one.

Tip: Co-scheduled sections (same start time and duration on the same day) share a single tile on the Schedule Grid, stacked with a thin rule between them. This is normal and intentional.

Copying & Deleting Sections

Copying a section

Copying is useful when you need to create a second section of the same course with a similar setup.

  1. Select the section in the Section View list.
  2. Click the Copy button in the panel header.
  3. A new section is added immediately below the original, pre-filled with the same course. The section code is auto-incremented if possible (e.g. A → B, AB1 → AB2).
  4. If the incremented code is already taken, a notice appears and the code is left blank — you will need to enter one manually before saving.
  5. The new section opens for editing. Adjust any fields as needed and click Save.

Deleting a section

  1. Select the section in the Section View list.
  2. Click the Delete button in the panel header.
  3. Confirm the deletion. This cannot be undone.

Sorting Sections

The Section View list can be sorted three ways. Right-click on the Section View panel header (the blue strip at the top of the left panel) to see the sort options:

  • Subject/Course Code (default) — alphabetical by course code.
  • Instructor — alphabetical by lead instructor's first name. Unassigned sections sort to the end.
  • Section Type — alphabetical by section type. Unassigned sections sort to the end.

The active sort is shown with a ✓ in the menu and also displayed in small text beside the panel header. Your preference is saved and remembered.

The Schedule Grid

The Schedule Grid (right panel) is the visual heart of the application. It shows a weekly calendar with Monday through Friday as columns and time of day (08:30 to 22:00) on the vertical axis. Each section with scheduled meeting times appears as a tile on the grid.

Reading the tiles

Each tile shows the course code, section code, and instructor initials on a single line — for example: HIST101 A JRS. When two sections share the exact same time slot, they are stacked vertically within the tile, separated by a thin horizontal rule.

When sections overlap (different start or end times), they appear side by side within the day column, each sized proportionally to its duration.

Zooming

At the bottom of the grid is a zoom slider. Drag it left to see more of the week at once, or right to zoom in for a more detailed view. The current zoom level is shown as a percentage.

Cross-panel selection

Clicking a tile in the Schedule Grid selects the corresponding section in the Section View list and highlights the instructor in the Workload View — and vice versa. All three panels stay in sync.

Using Filters

Filters let you focus the Schedule Grid on just the sections you care about. The filter bar runs along the top of the grid panel.

The eight filter dimensions

Instructors Rooms Subject Campus Section Type Tags Meeting Type Level

Click any button in the filter bar to open a popup for that dimension. Check one or more items to restrict the grid to matching sections. The logic works like this:

  • Within a dimension (e.g. multiple instructors selected): sections matching any of them are shown (OR logic).
  • Across dimensions (e.g. a subject filter AND an instructor filter both active): only sections matching both are shown (AND logic).

When any filter is active, an amber badge appears on the filter bar to remind you the view is restricted. Click Clear All at the right end of the filter bar to remove all filters at once.

Special sentinels

In the Instructors filter you will see a special item called Not staffed at the top of the list. Select it to show only sections that have no instructor assigned. Similarly, Unroomed in the Rooms filter shows sections with no room assigned. These sentinel items are mutually exclusive with named items — you can't combine "Not staffed" with specific instructor names.

Example: To see all lectures on campus taught by a specific instructor, open the Instructors filter and check that instructor, then open the Section Type filter and check "Lecture", then open the Campus filter and check the relevant campus. Only sections matching all three conditions will appear.

Overlays

Overlays are different from filters. Instead of hiding sections, an overlay highlights matching sections in red — on top of whatever the normal filtered view shows. This makes it easy to spot one instructor's or room's sections in the context of the full schedule.

Overlay buttons appear to the right of the main filter buttons in the filter bar, after a separator:

  • Instructor overlay — highlight all sections for a chosen instructor.
  • Room overlay — highlight all sections meeting in a chosen room.
  • Tag overlay — highlight all sections with a chosen tag.

Activating an overlay

  1. Click the overlay button (Instructor, Room, or Tag). A popup opens.
  2. Click the item you want to highlight. It is marked with a checkmark.
  3. The overlay activates — matching tiles turn red on the grid. A note in the panel header shows the overlay is active.
  4. Click the same item again (or click elsewhere) to deactivate the overlay.
Overlay vs Filter: Use a filter when you want to hide everything else and focus. Use an overlay when you want to see one instructor's or room's load in the context of the whole schedule — for example, to spot potential overloads or room conflicts visually.

Quick Assignment (Right-click on Grid)

You can assign instructors, rooms, and tags directly from the Schedule Grid without opening the full section editor. This is handy for quick updates when you're reviewing the grid.

  1. Right-click any section tile on the grid. A small context menu appears with three options: Assign Instructor, Assign Room, Assign Tag.
  2. Click the type of assignment you want to make.
  3. A panel opens on the right side of the menu:
    • Instructors — checkboxes; you can assign multiple.
    • Room — radio-button list; select one room for the specific meeting you clicked.
    • Tags — checkboxes; you can assign multiple.
  4. Make your selections and click OK. The grid and section list both update immediately.
Note: When assigning a room from the grid, the assignment applies to the specific meeting time (day and start time) you right-clicked, not to all meetings in the section.

Copying a Semester

The Copy Semester feature lets you duplicate all sections from one semester into another — useful for rolling over a schedule to the next year.

  1. Click Academic Years in the menu bar.
  2. Click Copy Semester. The copy dialog opens.
  3. Under From, choose the academic year and semester you want to copy.
  4. Under To, choose the destination academic year and semester.
  5. Use the checkboxes to control what gets copied:
    • Copy section designations only — copies course + section code only (the bare minimum).
    • Copy section designations and: — copies designations plus anything you check: section types, tags, instructors, meeting times (with optional room and meeting-type sub-options), reserves.
  6. Click Copy.

Incompatible schedules

If you are copying meeting times to a different academic year that has different legal start times configured, the app checks each section. Any section whose meeting times don't fit the destination year's schedule is listed in a warning screen. You can:

  • Click Abort to cancel the copy entirely.
  • Click Continue to proceed — flagged sections are copied without their meeting times. You can optionally export a CSV report of the flagged sections.

Emptying a Semester

The Empty Semester feature deletes all sections from a chosen semester. Use this if you need to start a semester from scratch.

Warning: Emptying a semester permanently deletes all sections in it. This cannot be undone. Consider copying the semester first if you want a backup.
  1. Click Academic Years in the menu bar.
  2. Click Empty Semester.
  3. Choose the academic year and then the semester you want to empty. The number of sections that will be deleted is shown.
  4. Click Empty Semester to confirm.
