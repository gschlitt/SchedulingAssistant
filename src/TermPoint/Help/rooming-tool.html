Using TermPoint's' Rooming Tool

This applies to users who are assigned rooms in advance which they must use for their sections. It assumes the user has supplied the unit's room types, rooms and campuses (under 'Scheduling Environment'.)

Early-phase rooming

Note that in the early phases of scheduling, it's better and faster to use the Unroomed Filter and the 'Overlay' tool to assign rooms. Assuming you have a number of sections scheduled, and need to find rooms:

  • Use the 'Room' Filter to filter by 'unroomed' sections
  • Choose a room and overlay it on the schedule view. Shown framed in red are the times when the room is booked.
  • Find sections which do not conflict with the booked times, right-click and assign them to the section. You can continue to fill up that room as much as possible.
  • Now continue with another room until all sections have been roomed.

Later-phase rooming

At later stages of timetabling, most of the sections have been roomed, and it is more convenient to use the room finder tool. Assuming you have a section which does not yet have rooms:

  • Edit the section, and specify the minimum requirements you have of the schedule. You may require that the meetings occur at certain times, but you do not need to do even this. (You might be willing to place the section wherever and whenever a room could be found). You must however specify the meeting lengths for the meetings you create.
  • Some examples:
    • You might want two meetings, on Monday and Friday, both 1.5 hours long, each in a 'small classroom' (one of your room types).
    • Or, your might want three meetings, two of length 1.5 hours of meeting type 'lecture' and one 3 hour meeting of type 'lab' (so that the days of the meeting don't matter). '
  • Note that if you leave something unspecified (a room type, say) then any room available is offered, even those of type you may not want.
  • Note that if the section has a capacity specified then only rooms of at least that capacity are offered.
